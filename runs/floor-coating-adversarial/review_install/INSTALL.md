# Install-Risk Review: Floor Coating (D010)

## Execution Risk Analysis

### 1. The Thin-Film "Marine" Trap (TotalTread, Interdeck, EZ-Decks)
Thin-film marine deck paints (TotalBoat, Interlux, Pettit) are a massive execution risk for a moto/cargo trailer. 
- **Gouge Risk:** 1–3 mil dry film will not survive a 500lb bike on a kickstand or the sliding of E-track fittings. 
- **PlexCore Adhesion:** These are designed for gelcoat or primed wood; they will likely soak into PlexCore unevenly, requiring expensive marine primers that erase the "bargain" price.
- **Verdict:** Rejected. These are "appearance" coatings for foot traffic, not cargo-ramp liners.

### 2. KiwiGrip (Water-Based Acrylic)
- **DIY Texture Risk:** Slip resistance depends entirely on the "KiwiGrip Roller" technique. Inconsistent rolling across 140 ft² will lead to "bald spots" and "peaks" that trap playa dust forever.
- **Fuel Tolerance:** Acrylics are the weakest against gasoline drips from a carb overflow or tank vent.
- **Verdict:** Rejected on cleanup and chemical risk.

### 3. Tuff Coat (Rubberized Middle Ground)
- **Dependency Risk:** Requires a specific $100/gal primer and a specific Tuff Coat roller. Missing one component stalls the build.
- **Cure Timing:** Water-based systems are slave to humidity. If the trailer is sealed too soon for the Juplaya deadline, it will remain soft and peel.
- **Verdict:** Viable only if Durabak fails the patch test; otherwise, it’s a more complex install for similar money.

### 4. Durabak-18 (Current Recommendation)
- **Cove Risk:** One-part urethanes can sag. Getting a uniform 4" cove without "pooling" at the floor-wall transition is a high-skill task.
- **Deadline Risk:** Moisture-cure urethanes need 3–5 days to reach full hardness. With Juplaya in July, any delay in the floor "sequences" the entire interior (FRP cannot land until the cove is cured).

### 1. Recommended Floor System
**U-POL Raptor 2K Polyurethane (Tintable or Grey).** 
Raptor's 2K chemistry provides a harder, faster cure than Durabak's one-part moisture-cure, which is critical for the July 4th deadline. It has superior adhesion to composite woods like PlexCore and lower "sag" risk on the 4" cove.

### 2. Posture: Why I Reject Durabak-18
Durabak is a "premium" name with one-part convenience that masks a high install risk. One-part urethanes are notoriously picky about humidity—too dry and they don't cure; too wet and they bubble. For a deadline-driven build, a 2K system (Raptor) offers predictable cure windows and higher "build" for the ramp transition.

### 3. Strongest Counterargument
"Raptor requires a compressor/Schutz gun or a high-effort roller mix, whereas Durabak is 'open and roll'." If the owner lacks air or prefers the convenience of a single-can product, Durabak is the only choice despite the cure-timing risks.

### 4. Required Patch Tests & Install Notes
- **PlexCore Adhesion:** 6"x6" patch test. Sand with 60-grit, vacuum, wipe with MEK. Apply coating. Wait 48 hours. Attempt to peel with a putty knife.
- **E-Track Masking:** Use 3M 233+ High-Performance Green tape. Mask the *inside* of the E-track slots and the bolt heads. If the coating bridges the track, you will never get a fitting to click in without a chisel.
- **Cove Detail:** Use a high-density foam "hot dog" roller for the 4" wall cove to prevent sagging. Land the FRP at least 24 hours *after* the cove is tack-free.

### 5. One-line Verdict for D010
**REPLACE** with U-POL Raptor 2K; it's cheaper, cures faster for the deadline, and is tougher for the ramp.
