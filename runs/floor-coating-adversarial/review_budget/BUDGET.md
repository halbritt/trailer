# Budget Review: D010 Floor Coating

## 1. Recommended floor system

Replace D010 with **3 gal Herculiner Original black roll-on truck bed liner** for the floor, 3-4" cove, and ramp, conditional on the same PlexCore adhesion and fuel-drip patch gates already required for Durabak. Home Depot's current signal is [$89.99/gal](https://www.homedepot.com/p/Herculiner-1-gal-Solid-Flat-Matte-Black-Truck-Bed-Liner-Kit-HCL1B8/327813191), so the base material buy is about **$270**. Herculiner's own page calls it a roll-on polyurethane/rubber coating that bonds to wood and is repairable, with 24 hour cure; Lowe's product page explicitly claims gasoline/oil/solvent/chemical resistance.

This is the cheapest system I will call acceptable. It is not the prettiest system, and black is inferior to light grey for heat, dust visibility, and interior brightness. Those are comfort/finish penalties, not mission killers. If black is unacceptable after the sample panel, the budget upgrade is **Raptor tintable/grey**, not Durabak by default.

Marine deck paints are not a magic cost escape. TotalTread, Interdeck, and EZ-Decks are useful boat-deck foot-traffic coatings, not cargo liners. They are thin enough that a bike chock, kickstand, dropped E-track fitting, or ramp scrape can cut through to PlexCore. Tuff Coat must be separated from that class: it is high-build and impact-resistant, but the 40-45 ft2/gal coverage plus bare-wood primer turns it into similar money with more install dependency. KiwiGrip is also not the answer: it is repairable, but it is acrylic, textured enough to hold playa dust in valleys, and its own FAQ says fuels/oils should not pool.

## 2. Why this posture rejects Durabak

Durabak survives the functional argument, but loses the budget argument. Its own current page lists $159.95/gal and about 60 ft2/gal for two coats; D010 needs 3 gal, so the real buy is still about **$480** before consumables. The evidence brief's $540 signal only strengthens the point. Herculiner buys the same broad truck-bed category, wood application, roll-on DIY workflow, cove compatibility, repairability, and spill-cleanup posture for roughly **$210-270 less**.

Durabak's actual advantage is convenience and finish: light grey, UV/outdoor colors, known coverage, no mixing, good texture control, and a cleaner story for the synthesizer. That is not free. For a floor that will be hidden under bikes, dust, chocks, and E-track fittings, paying nearly double for color and confidence is a luxury unless Herculiner fails the actual trailer patch.

## 3. Strongest counterargument

The best argument against Herculiner is that it is the cheap black truck-bed option for a reason. The black surface will run hotter, show playa dust harshly, darken the interior, and may be rough enough to hold dust in the texture. Durabak light grey is more owner-aligned and more visually forgiving, and Raptor tintable may be the better middle path if the owner will spend above the floor price.

That counterargument is real, but it is not enough to keep Durabak as D010. The stated requirements are fuel/oil spill tolerance, playa hose-out cleanup, cove tray behavior, and moto/ramp durability. Herculiner gets to test against those requirements before Durabak gets the premium.

## 4. Required patch tests and install notes

- **Adhesion patch:** sand actual PlexCore with 80 grit, vacuum hard, wipe per Herculiner instructions with xylene/acetone/MEK on a small controlled area, then roll a 6" x 12" two-coat patch. After 48 hours, crosshatch and pry with a putty knife. Any sheet peel means reject Herculiner.
- **Fuel/oil patch:** on a cured patch, test gasoline, diesel, and engine oil as realistic spills, not immersion. Leave each for 30-60 minutes, wipe promptly, scrub with soap/water, and reject if the coating softens, swells, loses adhesion, or stains into gummy residue.
- **Dust/hose patch:** grind in playa-dust proxy or real playa dust if available, wet it, let it dry, then hose and deck-brush it. Reject if the texture traps paste that will not release without aggressive scraping.
- **Cove patch:** brush/roll a 3-4" vertical cove sample at the wall/floor corner before FRP. Confirm no sag, no uncured pooling at the bend, and a clean FRP lap line after cure.
- **Gouge/repair patch:** after full cure, drag a chock foot/E-track fitting across the sample, then spot-repair. Herculiner says it adheres to itself; prove the repair edge bonds.
- **Install order:** finish recessed E-track layout and through-bolting first. Mask E-track slots, fastener heads, D-ring pockets, ramp hinge hardware, and any drain/edge details before coating. Do not stretch coverage; if the sample shows 3 gal is too tight, buy a fourth gallon rather than thinning or starving the ramp.

## 5. One-line verdict for D010

**REPLACE:** D010 should become "Herculiner 3 gal black roll-on truck-bed liner, patch-gated; Durabak only if Herculiner fails or black is an owner-level deal-breaker."
