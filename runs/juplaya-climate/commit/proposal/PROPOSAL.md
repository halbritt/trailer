# Climate / envelope — committed proposal (cross-examined)

Cleared by the collaboration ledger (cycle 1, verdict **accept_with_findings**).
These are the final committed recommendations for the four climate/envelope open
questions, incorporating the two findings the cross-examination forced. Settled
items not relitigated: closed-cell spray foam, elastomeric roof coating, Velit
Mini 2000R AC (48 VDC, ordered).

Frame: interior 81" W × 157" L × 78" tall; **vertical posts 16" o.c., 76.5" tube
posts, roof bows 24" o.c.**; single 3,500 lb nose-sensitive axle, **payload to be
weighed**; 48V LiTime stack (5.12 kWh, AIO in nose) with **no 12V system yet**;
Silverfrost ACP exterior + 1-piece aluminum roof; ready for Juplaya ~July 4 2026.

## Q8 — Windows  (CORRECTED after cross-exam — Finding 1)
**COMMITTED: Install ONE framed curbside awning window, NOT two. Size it to the
measured clear opening (target rough-opening width ≤ ~26"), spanning two 16"-o.c.
bays and cutting the single intermediate vertical tube post, with a MANDATORY
load-transfer sub-frame (welded/bolted header + sill + jack-studs) around the
opening. Curbside only (door/awning/living side); roadside wall stays
uninterrupted E-track.**

- Why corrected: the original "two windows, ~22" between posts" was geometrically
  impossible — 16" o.c. posts give ≤15" clear, and even a two-bay span nets only
  ~27.5" clear after jack-studs. One window halves the structural cut/cost.
- HARD PRE-PURCHASE MEASUREMENT: the actual clear opening after framing picks the
  window; do not order glass before measuring. Confirm no clearance light /
  wiring / factory flow-thru vent lands in the chosen bays.
- FALLBACK (operator's call): if cutting a load-bearing post is rejected, install
  **no glass** and add a **powered roof-vent fan** for ventilation instead. Either
  way, the personnel door + factory flow-thru vents remain the baseline airflow.
- FLAG purchase: 1× frameless tinted dual-pane RV awning window (sized to measured
  opening) + framing steel, OR a roof-vent fan if the fallback is chosen.

## Q9 — HRV  (HELD — uncontested)
**COMMITTED: Do NOT install an HRV for Juplaya. Rough-in now: cut + trim-ring two
paired 4" wall penetrations (fresh/stale, low/high) on the roadside wall, blank
with insulated plugs, and pull a labeled 24V/12V power string to that location. No
core, no ducting purchased now.**

- Rationale: July moto basecamp is dry and well-ventilated by door + window +
  flow-thru vents; the HRV's payoff is in sealed cold-weather / gas-adjacent
  profiles (mission #2/#4). Penetrate while the envelope is open (cheap now,
  destructive after foam + FRP); defer the device. Coordinate rough-in location
  with the wall E-track rows so the future core doesn't collide with a track.
- FLAG purchase (small, now): 2× 4" insulated wall plugs + trim rings + pull string.

## Q10 — Diesel heater  (DOWNGRADED after cross-exam — Finding 2)
**COMMITTED: Do NOT install the diesel heater for July. Adopt the same
"penetrate-now / defer-the-unit" posture as Q9: rough-in the through-floor exhaust
+ fuel-line penetrations (in a floor bay clear of crossmembers and E-track rows)
and pull the CO-detector + 12V power string NOW; DEFER the heater unit + external
tank to the cold-weather mission profiles. The hardwired 12V CO detector is a
COMMITMENT conditional on the heater ever being fired.**

- Why downgraded: cross-exam landed that mild ~50–60°F July Black Rock nights
  don't justify a permanent diesel install against under-floor-exhaust fire risk
  over dry scrub, the 48→12V conversion overhead (no 12V system exists yet), and a
  heavy fuel tank forward on a nose-sensitive single axle. A quilt + the
  closed-cell envelope cover July comfort. For July, no heater unit, no tank.
- When later adopted: floor-mounted sealed-combustion, exhaust straight down
  through the floor (never the wall), combustion air from outside, dedicated small
  external diesel tank (NOT plumbed from the F-150), CO/combo detector live first.
- FLAG cross-run dependency: the eventual heater's 12V glow-plug/fan draw (~8–10 A
  startup) is a real **12V load** → hands a requirement to the systems run (Q4 load
  list, Q3 48→12V converter sizing). Even pre-install, the CO detector + any 12V
  string raises the 48→12V converter question for that run.
- FLAG purchase (now): through-floor exhaust + fuel pass-through fittings, 12V CO/
  combo detector, CO-detector wiring. Heater + tank: deferred.

## Q11 — Awning  (HELD — uncontested; IN SCOPE for July)
**COMMITTED: Install a ~12 ft manual lateral-arm roll-out RV awning
(Carefree/Dometic-class) on the CURBSIDE, through-bolted to the aluminum top rail
via an aluminum mounting rail / Z-bracket with backing plates — NEVER fastened to
the ACP skin or 1-piece aluminum roof alone. Manual (no 12V motor) for redundancy.
Leading edge clears the 32"×72" personnel-door swing; include positive lock-down /
de-flap straps for desert gusts.**

- Rationale: the required July deliverable and the biggest livability multiplier
  (shaded dry outdoor room at the door). Manual = no motor/controller to fail
  remotely, zero 12V draw. ~12 ft matches the 13'1" body without overhanging the
  V-nose. Co-locates shade + door + the curbside window into one living face.
- FLAG coordination (systems run): curbside top-rail real estate is shared with
  the **solar side-rail mounts** (systems Q2). Sequence the awning rail and solar
  rail so they co-exist on the same top rail.
- FLAG measurement: curbside wall height + top-rail profile; door-head height vs.
  awning rail; rolled-box added width vs. the 8'6" legal envelope.
- FLAG purchase: ~12 ft manual lateral-arm RV awning + curbside through-bolt
  mounting rail + backing plates + tie-down kit.
