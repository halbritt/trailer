# L-Track Floor Chock Options

Purpose: document the switch from floor E-track to floor L-track for the motorcycle chock/tie-down layout.

## Decision Lean

Use **floor L-track** for the motorcycle floor system. Keep **wall E-track** for bed/shelf shoring, panel transport, fridge restraint, and general wall modules.

Starting floor geometry: **two lengthwise L-track rows, about 26 in on center**, with final placement re-derived after the bike/chock/steel measurement pass.

Order status: **two 8 ft flanged L-track sections are on the way** (2026-06-05). **Bolt It On 360 L-track wheel chocks are ordered** (2026-06-05). Remaining buys/checks are the exact stud/quick-release hardware, backing/fasteners, and final row stationing.

Why:

- L-track is lower-profile and better under a camp floor/sleeping-deck use case.
- The floor only needs a couple of motorcycle wheel locators and tie-down points, not the full E-track lumber-socket ecosystem.
- L-track chocks exist for dirt bikes, dual sports, sport bikes, and e-bikes.
- Existing E-track still has a good home on the walls, where the 2x4 shoring sockets and broad accessory ecosystem matter.

## Install Implications

- Recompute the final floor rows from the 26 in-on-center L-track starting layout after measuring floor steel, chock hardware, front-tire centerlines, and bike handlebar width.
- The track should still be recessed/flush enough to avoid a toe-stub and bed-platform bump.
- Bolt through to steel/backing wherever the load path requires it; do not rely on PlexCore alone for motorcycle restraint.
- Mask L-track slots and fasteners before Durabak so fittings remain usable.
- Add a third Durabak coat in ramp, chock, and L-track wear lanes.
- Keep the four factory 5,000 lb D-rings as backup tie points.
