# Floor-Coating Evidence Brief — 2026-06-05

Purpose: source pack for the 3-way adversarial review of D010 flooring. The owner likes Durabak's durability/weight, but the cost is substantial; this brief gives reviewers the same baseline facts.

## Trailer Scope

- Interior floor bound: 81" × 157" = ~88 ft².
- 3–4" cove up both long walls plus front/nose/rear edges: ~12–14 ft².
- Rear ramp deck: 78" × 72" = ~39 ft².
- Floor+cove+ramp coating scope: ~140 ft² before patch tests, masking loss, roller loss, and touch-up margin.
- Required behavior: moto fuel/oil spill tolerance, playa dust cleanup, hose-out tray behavior, enough grip for wet/dusty boots and tires, no loose sheet that traps alkali dust/water against PlexCore.

## Claims To Stress-Test

1. **Durabak premium:** Does $540 buy enough extra durability, UV stability, cove quality, color choice, and DIY controllability to beat Raptor or Herculiner?
2. **Raptor economics:** Is one 4L kit + one 1L bottle a defensible lower-cost substitute, or does the tight coverage and mixing/spray workflow make it false economy?
3. **Herculiner bargain:** Is 3 gal Herculiner good enough for a trailer floor at half the Durabak price, or do black-only color/texture/UV/finish concerns make it a bad fit?
4. **Rust-Oleum floor:** Is the very low cost worth considering, or is water-based styrenated acrylic disqualified for moto/playa floor duty?
5. **G-Floor fallback:** Is the glued PVC roll actually a better cost/value answer despite higher material cost, or does it lose on seams/cove/weight/trapped edges?
